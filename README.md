# **customer-category-classification-using-machine-learning**
This project focuses on automating customer classification for a wholesale food distributor. The goal was to segment customers into HORECA (Hotels, Restaurants, Cafés) and Retail (Grocery shops and supermarkets) based on purchasing patterns across categories such as Fresh, Milk, Grocery, Frozen, and Detergents & Paper.
# **Key Findings**

- HORECA customers purchase large quantities of Fresh and Frozen items.

- Retail customers buy more Milk, Grocery packets, and Detergents & Paper.

- Spending patterns reveal clear differentiators that can guide marketing and inventory decisions.

# **Methodology**

**1) Data Cleaning & Exploratory Analysis**

- Used histograms, boxplots, and pairplots for analysis (via a custom data analysis web app).

**2) Model Building**

- Trained a Gaussian Naive Bayes classifier to segment customers.

**3) Evaluation**

- Metrics: Accuracy, Precision, Recall, F1-score

- Confusion Matrix analysis to identify misclassifications

# **Business Insights**

- **Retail predictions:** High precision ensures marketing campaigns target the right shops, minimizing wasted spend.

- **HORECA predictions:** High recall ensures large-volume customers are correctly identified, protecting revenue opportunities.

- **Key Features:** Grocery, Milk, and Fresh items are most influential for classification.

- **Impact:** Enables targeted offers, optimized inventory planning, and improved ROI.

# **Conclusion**

This solution allows the business to automatically segment customers, improving operational efficiency, marketing effectiveness, and strategic decision-making.
